The birthplace of the electric telephone

The birthplace of the electric telephone, Brantford became one of Ontario’s industrial centres. In the ‘80s and ‘90s, that all fell apart and Brantford was seen as a city in collapse. The city turned it around with Highway 403 though. Today, Brantford has positioned itself as the mid-point between Toronto and Hamilton, wooing companies with lower rents, but easy access to the big cities. That approach has brought a lot of manufacturing – and headquarters – to the area… and a ton of jobs along with them! Combine the number of jobs with the cheaper housing available in the area and you have a city that it’s possible to make a life in.

Housing Market

Brantford is considered one of the hottest housing markets in all of Canada. With the city becoming more and more in demand as housing prices soar in Toronto and Hamilton, people are fleeing to the suburbs, especially Brantford. That means Brantford rental listings prices are likely to jump over the next few years. Look out for affordable 1, 2, 3, bedroom apartments for rent Brantford, houses for rent in Brantford, and condos for rent in Brantford. Given that, if you are interested in the area, now would be a good time to get houses for rent Brantford and Brantford apartments and rent!

Typical housing type:

New, suburban homes and chic apartments and condos.

Side note:

If you’re all about the suburbs, Brantford is a great choice. It’s close to Toronto and Hamilton. There are lots of entertainment options, and the schools are pretty solid too. The only question is if you want that suburban environment… because Brantford is the epitome of the suburbs.

Residents

Brantford tends to draw folks who can’t or don’t want to pay the sky-high rents found in Toronto and Hamilton. Families abound in the area – and single parents aren’t unusual. Unlike many family-oriented towns where it would be hard to date, there are a lot of divorcees and widowed locals in Brantford. In fact, there are so many people looking for love that there are regular speed dating events! When not making time for socializing, locals tend to be busy. They are so focused on family, work and school that there isn’t a very strong sense community in the area.

Perfect for: Single parents looking for a place with good jobs and deals on housing.

Not-so-perfect for: Anyone who isn’t into that suburban vibe.

Life & Style

Life in Brantford is full of suburban go, go, go. There’s that suburban vibe where the city is too sprawling to really have a sense of community and too constructed to have little neighbourhoods popping up. That’s fine with residents, though, as they tend to focus on their work, their homes and their kids. Community just isn’t a top priority. If anything, when locals have downtime, they’re more likely to relax at home or head into Toronto or Hamilton than hang out in Brantford.

Neighbourhood

Brantford is a transportation hub with a number of highways and major roads intersecting in it. On the western side of the city is the Grand River, which is surrounded with beautiful parks. You can also find golf clubs in this part of the city. There is one hospital – Brantford General. You’ll have plenty of school options and places of worship. In the north end, there are a variety of entertainment spots, as well as a mall and a Walmart. For restaurants, head to the centre of town. Kitchener, Waterloo, Cambridge, and Guelph are all within driving distance. Hamilton is also close – and offers up everything from an art gallery to an airport.
